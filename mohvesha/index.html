Six things working against you — even while you're job-hunting.

What this costs you: Every recruiter and patient search stops before they even click your profile. You disappear in the feed.
02
Problem Two

Your URL is still auto-generated.

/dr-mohvesha-mohammed-265b463a2 — that string at the end is the placeholder LinkedIn assigns when a profile is never claimed. Both Google search and the algorithm read this as a signal you don't take your profile seriously.

You've signaled availability to recruiters. But your profile doesn't specify whether you want clinical, corporate wellness, preventive medicine research, integrative care clinics, or teaching. That banner raised a flag — and the flag is blank.

3 certifications announced in 60 days — NIN internship, Mental Wellbeing, Cupping Therapy. All auto-posts. The algorithm categorizes profiles that only publish system-generated content as "not creators." Your organic reach is pinned near zero.

Naturopathy is a profession where the why matters as much as the credentials. Patients choose naturopaths on belief and trust — not just qualifications. Without an About, there's no space for the story, POV, or patient philosophy that earns that trust before the first consultation.

What this costs you: You're reduced to a category. And naturopathy has a lot of people in that category.

Why Most BNYS Doctors Stay Invisible

It's not that they don't try. It's that each of these is hard alone.

You've probably already noticed some of the gaps above. Most doctors do. The problem isn't identification — it's execution. These six frictions are why almost nobody does this alone.

Friction One

The Consistency Trap

LinkedIn's algorithm needs 3–4 posts/week for 4–6 months before it reclassifies you as a creator. That's 80+ posts, most while you're starting a clinical career. Most candidates stop at post 10.

Friction Two

The Voice Problem

Preventive and lifestyle medicine is a crowded niche — thousands of BNYS doctors post similar content. Differentiation requires a POV you haven't named yet. You can't name it in isolation. It gets extracted, not invented.

Friction Three

The Angle Commitment

Clinical private practice. Corporate wellness. Hospital integrative care. Research. Teaching. Content. BNYS can lead to 6 different careers. Most new doctors never publicly commit to one — and hedging shows in every post.

Friction Four

The Network Math

138 → 5,000 connections isn't about mass-connecting. Every wrong connection dilutes your feed quality and your credibility. Strategic network growth requires a list, a reason to connect, and a cadence — not a connect-all spree.

Friction Five

The Algorithm Penalty

Right now, LinkedIn suppresses your posts because you're classified as inactive. Reversing that requires consistency + quality + engagement simultaneously — not one at a time. Miss any leg and the climb resets.

Friction Six

The Identity Question

"A naturopath" is a category. "The naturopath known for [X]" is a career. But X has to be something you can actually deliver, claim publicly, and defend with proof. Picking X alone, without a strategist, is why most doctors settle for the category.
